Afrobeat

Description

Afrobeat is a vibrant fusion of traditional African rhythms with elements of jazz, funk, highlife, and soul. Originating in Nigeria in the late 1960s, Afrobeat was pioneered by the legendary Fela Kuti, who used the genre to address political and social issues in his lyrics.

Key Characteristics

  • Complex, polyrhythmic percussion.
  • Heavy use of horn sections for melodic and harmonic lines.
  • Call-and-response vocals, often with a social or political message.
  • Long, improvisational instrumentals.

History

The genre emerged during a time of political unrest in Nigeria. Fela Kuti, alongside drummer Tony Allen, combined traditional Nigerian highlife and Yoruba music with influences from American jazz and funk. Afrobeat quickly became a medium for resistance, spreading across Africa and the diaspora.

Prominent Artists

  • Tems
  • Ruger
  • Fela Kuti
  • Burna Boy
